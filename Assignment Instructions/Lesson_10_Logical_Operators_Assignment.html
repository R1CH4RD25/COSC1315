üí° Tips for Success

Understanding Logical Operators:

  • and: Both conditions must be True (think “strict requirements”)

  • or: At least one condition must be True (think “flexible options”)

  • not: Inverts the boolean value (True ‚Üí False, False ‚Üí True)

Testing Your Logic:

  • Try different combinations of True/False values

  • Make a mental truth table for complex conditions

  • Use print statements to check intermediate values

  • Ask yourself: “Do I need both conditions or just one?”

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Confusing and with or (think about the scenario carefully)

  • Forgetting that not only applies to what comes right after it

  • Missing parentheses in complex expressions: (A or B) and C is different from A or (B and C)

  • Using = instead of == for comparisons
